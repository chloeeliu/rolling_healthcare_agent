General ICU surveillance:
- This is rolling monitoring at the current checkpoint, not future prediction.
- Use only data visible up to the current t_hour.
- suspected_conditions means clinically meaningful concern that should stay on the monitoring list.
- alerts means high-acuity or high-confidence states that justify escalation now.

Infection:
- Treat infection as an episode-level state once credible infection evidence appears.
- Culture support strengthens the case but is not required for infection suspicion.

Sepsis:
- Treat sepsis as infection plus clinically important organ dysfunction.
- Escalate to sepsis alert when infection is present and organ dysfunction evidence is strong, especially with Sepsis-3 style evidence or shock physiology.

Renal:
- AKI should be interpreted by KDIGO stage severity, and the benchmark remembers the worst stage reached so far.
- Oliguria is a rolling-window short-horizon signal and may appear or expire over time.
- CRRT indicates active renal support rather than a permanent state.

Respiratory:
- HFNC or NIV is a meaningful support-escalation state.
- Invasive ventilation is alert-level respiratory support.
- PF ratio, oxygenation, lactate, and pH are recent physiologic measurements and can expire if not refreshed.

Hemodynamic:
- Any vasoactive support is a strong hemodynamic instability signal.
- Multi-agent or heavier support increases severity.
- Shock-like states depend on combined evidence such as sepsis, support exposure, and metabolic derangement.

Neurologic:
- GCS <= 8 is a severe neurologic deterioration signal.
- GCS states should be interpreted as recent observed neurologic status, not permanent once-on labels.

Metabolic:
- Lactate >= 2 suggests important metabolic stress; lactate >= 4 is a severe alert-level derangement.
- pH < 7.30 suggests acidemia; pH <= 7.20 is severe acidemia.

Coagulation:
- INR >= 1.5 is a meaningful coagulation abnormality.
- INR >= 2.0 is a stronger alert-level coagulopathy signal.
