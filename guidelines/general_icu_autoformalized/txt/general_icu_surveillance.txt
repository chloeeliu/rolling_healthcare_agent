General ICU surveillance benchmark guidance.

- This is a rolling monitoring task at the current checkpoint, not a future prediction task.
- Use only data visible up to the current t_hour.
- Start broad, then narrow: check likely active organ-support or deterioration families before committing to alerts.
- suspected_conditions means clinically meaningful concern that should stay on the monitoring list.
- alerts means high-acuity or high-confidence states that justify escalation now.
- global_action should be continue_monitoring when no current alert-level state is active and escalate when one or more alert-level states are active.
- priority should usually be low for no active alerts, medium for important suspect states or a single moderate alert, and high for severe or multi-system alert states.
- Use guideline search to clarify condition thresholds and use function search to discover relevant autoformalized concepts.
- Keep checkpoint_summary very short so it can be reused as rolling memory at the next checkpoint.
