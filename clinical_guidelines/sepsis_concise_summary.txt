Sepsis Diagnosis (Sepsis-3) – Concise Summary

Definition:
Sepsis is a life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by a dysregulated host response to infection.

Core Diagnostic Criteria:
1. Suspected or confirmed infection
2. Evidence of organ dysfunction:
   - Operationalized as an increase in SOFA score ≥ 2

Clinical Interpretation:
- SOFA ≥ 2 indicates significant risk of mortality (~10%)
- Reflects multi-organ involvement (respiratory, cardiovascular, renal, liver, coagulation, CNS)

Screening (outside ICU):
qSOFA ≥ 2 suggests high risk of poor outcomes:
- Respiratory rate ≥ 22/min
- Altered mentation
- Systolic BP ≤ 100 mmHg

Septic Shock (subset of sepsis):
- Requires vasopressors to maintain MAP ≥ 65 mmHg
- Lactate > 2 mmol/L despite adequate fluid resuscitation
- Associated with >40% mortality

Key Concepts:
- Sepsis ≠ infection alone; requires organ dysfunction
- SIRS criteria are insufficient (low specificity/sensitivity)
- Early recognition and intervention are critical
- No single gold-standard diagnostic test exists

Practical EHR Implementation:
- Identify infection via antibiotics + culture timing
- Track SOFA score dynamically (e.g., hourly or 4-hour windows)
- Use qSOFA for rapid bedside screening
- Escalate to full evaluation when criteria met
